My book _Music in the Flesh: An Early Modern Musical Physiology_ (Chicago, 2023) reimagines the lived experiences of people making music—composers, performers, listeners—in seventeenth- and early eighteenth-century Europe. It explores how music affected them in body, soul and spirit. 

There are countless historical testimonies describing the powerful effects of music on the body. My book explores musical, philosophical, anatomical, medical, theological and devotional writings of the time to show that these historical bodies were porous, flowing, ensouled, and easily permeated and transformed by musical sound.  

These early modern experiences challenge our modern-day understanding of human nature as a mind-body dichotomy. Instead, they affirm a more integrated, holistic vision of human nature, in which body, soul and spirit are inextricably entangled.
