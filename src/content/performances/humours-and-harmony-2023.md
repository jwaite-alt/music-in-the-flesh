## About this performance

*Humours and Harmony* was a pilot performance held at Emmanuel College, Cambridge, in May 2023. The event brought together a small ensemble of early music performers with an invited audience of musicologists, historians of medicine, and interested members of the public.

### Context

The performance grew out of Bettina Varwig's research into the physiological theories that underpinned early modern music-making. In the seventeenth century, music was understood not as an abstract art form but as a force that acted directly upon the body — altering the balance of the humours, exciting or calming the spirits, and moving the listener in a literal, physical sense.

### Programme

The programme focused on German sacred and domestic music of the early seventeenth century, with particular attention to works whose texts explicitly engage with bodily and spiritual transformation. Performers were asked to reflect on the physical demands of their instruments and voices as part of the performance preparation.

### Notes

This performance was not recorded. Documentation consists of participant notes and post-event discussion transcripts.
